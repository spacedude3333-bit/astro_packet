# Gravity



## Newton’s Law of Gravitation

F = G(m1m2 / r^2)



Meaning:

- gravitational force between two masses



Variables:

- F = force

- G = gravitational constant

- m1, m2 = masses

- r = distance between centers



Notes:

- inverse-square law

- larger r means much weaker force



---



# Circular Motion



## Centripetal acceleration

a = v^2 / r



## Centripetal force

F = mv^2 / r



---



# Orbital Motion



## Circular orbital speed

v = sqrt(GM / r)



Meaning:

- approximate speed for circular orbit around mass M



## Escape velocity

v = sqrt(2GM / r)



Meaning:

- minimum speed needed to escape gravity without more propulsion



---



# Radiation / Stars



## Wien’s Law

lambda max = b / T



Meaning:

- hotter objects peak at shorter wavelengths



## Stefan–Boltzmann Law

L = 4πR^2σT^4



Meaning:

- luminosity depends on radius and temperature
